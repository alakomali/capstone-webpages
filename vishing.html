What is Vishing?

Vishing, short for voice phishing, is a type of social engineering attack where an attacker uses phone calls or voice messages to deceive victims into revealing personal information, such as credit card numbers, bank account details, or other confidential data. The attacker typically impersonates a trusted individual or organization, such as a bank representative, government agency, or tech support personnel, to gain the victim‚Äôs trust. Vishing attacks rely on manipulation and urgency, pressuring the victim to act quickly and provide sensitive information over the phone.

Unlike smishing, which uses text messages, vishing exploits the human tendency to trust phone calls, particularly when the caller presents themselves as someone in a position of authority or expertise. Vishing can be carried out using either live phone calls or automated voice messages (also known as robocalls).

How Vishing Works

    Vishing attacks usually follow a few common steps designed to manipulate the victim:

  • Impersonation: The most common and typical example is the attacker claims to be from a recognizable and reliable source like a bank, government or technology support. They may even fake the number to make sure that the number on the ID is genuine.
  • Urgent Request: The attacker sets the victim in haste indicating that he had his/her account; hacked or that the account needs updating to avoid some set penalty or suspension.
  • Request for Sensitive Information: The attacker requests the victim‚Äôs personal information such as Social Security number, bank account, or credit card as part of identification or record update.
  • Follow-Up Actions: In some cases the attacker may trick the victim into calling the fraud support number or give further details in defending the breach, which also increases the odds of data theft.

Real-World Examples of Vishing Attacks

1. The IRS Vishing Scam

For instance, a senior citizen gets a call from someone who introduces himself as an IRS agent. The caller told the victim that he/she did not pay back taxes that had been withheld, and said that he/she would be arrested if the victim failed to pay back immediately.
